Conditions & Outlooks

A weekly set of supporting products show the inputs the USDM authors review when drawing the map — precipitation, streamflow, soil moisture, vegetation health, and forecast outlooks from the Climate Prediction Center.

🌡

Current Conditions

Composite drought indicators, percentiles, and short- and long-term assessments published weekly.

Open Current Conditions →
📅

Weekly Drought Indices

SPI, SPEI, PDSI, EDDI, and other standard indices, updated weekly for use by USDM authors.

Open Weekly Drought Indices →
🔭

Outlooks

The Climate Prediction Center's 6–10 day, 8–14 day, monthly and seasonal drought outlooks.

Open Outlooks →
🌧

Precipitation Analysis

NWS-AHPS precipitation analyses for 7-, 14-, 30-, 60-, 90-day and longer windows.

Open Precipitation Analysis →
🌾

Vegetation Drought Response (VegDRI)

Satellite-based assessment of vegetation stress combining VHI with climate and biophysical data.

Open VegDRI →
🏔

Mountain Snowpack

SNOTEL snow water equivalent percentiles across the western U.S. mountain ranges.

Open Mountain Snowpack →
🌱

Soil Moisture

NASA SPoRT, CPC-Leaky, and NLDAS soil-moisture percentiles across surface and root-zone depths.

Open Soil Moisture →
🌊

Streamflow

USGS daily streamflow percentiles and 7-day, 14-day, 28-day average flow conditions nationwide.

Open Streamflow →

The U.S. Drought Monitor is produced through a partnership between the National Drought Mitigation Center at the University of Nebraska–Lincoln, the United States Department of Agriculture, the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration and the National Aeronautics and Space Administration.
